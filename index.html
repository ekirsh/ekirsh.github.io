Since you've been on this page

Is it legal?

Section 215 of the Patriot Act gives the NSA authority to seize any relevant thing relating to an intelligence investigation. But is monitoring the phone calls and emails of millions of Americans relevant? The NSA argues that everything is relevant.

We will find out what senators think soon as Section 215 of the Patriot Act is set to expire. Some senators are wanting complete reauthorization of the law, but others are wanting a version that will limit NSA surveillance.

What are they doing?

The two major programs the NSA is running is PRISM and Upstream Data Collection (which is just a term for a bunch of different programs). PRISM is a NSA surveillance program that actually gets data from the servers of sites such as Google, Facebook and Yahoo. Upstream refers to the process of intercepting and copying relevant communications such as emails, phone calls and texts. A top secret NSA map shows they planted over 50000 viruses in foreign countries, even friendly countries.

The NSA knows almost everything about people based on what they send. As shown on the images above, the NSA access to your photos, videos and even your live video chats.
